Bruce lee

The Dragon's life

Timeline

1940: Born on November 27th in San Francisco, California.

1941: Moved with his family to Hong Kong.

1946: Began training in Wing Chun at the age of six under the tutelage of Yip Man.

1954: Won the Crown Colony Cha Cha Championship in Hong Kong at the age of 13. 1957: Participated in and won the Hong Kong Boxing Championship at the age of 17.

1959:Was arrested for fighting and decided to move to the United States.

1959-1964: Lived and taught martial arts in Seattle, Washington, where he opened his first martial arts school.

1964: Moved to Oakland, California to open a new martial arts school. This timeline details the key events that shaped Bruce Lee's early life and set the foundation for his future success as a martial artist, actor, and cultural icon.

1965: Lee married Linda Emery and they had two children, Brandon and Shannon Lee.

1969: Lee appeared in his first film, "The Green Hornet," as Kato, the sidekick to the title character.

1971: Lee's martial arts skills and charismatic screen presence caught the attention of Hollywood, and he was offered several film roles. He starred in four Hong Kong films, including "The Big Boss" and "Fists of Fury," which were huge box-office hits.

1973: Lee began working on his final film, "Enter the Dragon," which was released posthumously after his death.

July 20, 1973: Lee died suddenly at the age of 32 from a cerebral edema.

Lee's impact on martial arts and popular culture continues to this day, and he is widely regarded as one of the greatest martial artists of all time.
